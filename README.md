# Recommendations for Business Growth:
Target Young Shoppers (18-30 Age Group)

Since they spend the most, consider launching exclusive discounts, promotions, and marketing campaigns tailored to this segment.
Offer trendy and budget-friendly products to match their spending habits.
Increase Engagement for the 60+ Age Group

This group spends the least, so incentives like loyalty programs, special discounts, or personalized promotions may encourage more purchases.
Ensure the online shopping experience is simple and accessible for older customers.
Enhance High-Value Customer Retention

Since a small group of customers make high-value purchases, implement VIP memberships, premium offerings, and personalized services to encourage repeat spending.
Encourage bulk purchases by offering discounts at spending thresholds ($500, $1000).
Optimize Product Strategies

Clothing, Electronics, and Beauty all generate significant revenue, so maintaining a balanced inventory across these categories is crucial.
Since Clothing is the best-selling category, consider expanding product variety and introducing seasonal collections.
Leverage Gender-Based Marketing

Female shoppers dominate spending, especially in Beauty; consider exclusive product bundles or beauty-related offers for women.
Men are more inclined towards Electronics, so targeting them with tech deals, discounts, or gadget bundles could boost sales.
Expand Mid-Range Price Offerings

The most popular price points are $25, $30, $50, $300, and $500.
Consider launching more mid-range products within these price ranges, as they match customers' preferred spending habits.
Improve Customer Retention Strategies

Implement personalized recommendations, loyalty rewards, and targeted promotions to encourage repeat purchases.
Use email marketing and push notifications to remind customers about deals based on their past spending behavior.
